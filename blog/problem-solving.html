Problem Solving

Tech - Sprint 4 - 14/4/2024 - Dev Academy Foundations

This last week has been a mental doosey for me,
I went in feeling confident for another week of learning, sure that while I can't remember things off the top of my head right now, it wouldn't be an issue later; I'd get it eventually, I just need to keep working on it.
Near the middle, when I started one of our modules, JavaScript Kata, I realized that even once I resorted to using Gemini's AI to explain the questions in different ways, it sounded like French and made zero sense.

All of a sudden, nothing made sense. I felt stupid. I struggled with every single one of them.
For example, in the second series of questions “manipulating objects”
Looking back, there's probably a word for this type of coding where you don't add to the function within the function but call it instead.
I didn't think to look for that at the time, as the first one already had me feeling stupid.

Each question on the document we were given asked something like:

“// 7. Add a children key to terah and use object literal notation to assign this variable to an empty object.”

This sounded like french to me still, and by this point I had remembered what a “key” was.

It's a way of storing small bits of information, for example, the Date: 6th of Jan, Time: 8pm pieces of information that are often on event invitations.

I still had no idea what “object literal notation” was and reading “variable to an empty object.” made my brain feel like it was short circuiting.

I googled “object literal notation” and got more french sounding sentences; even asking Gemini to explain what it was wasn't helpful. Now I'm stressed.

Go for a walk to the kitchen, get some water, go back to it, and read it again.

Same helpless feeling. At this point, I want to give up and go back to sewing.
I know how to do that; it's really fun. I can make things that I can actually touch and feel in the finished items. It makes me happy. I never feel stressed or stupid when I'm sewing, it's easy to say not yet when I can feel and see with my own eyes exactly what's wrong.

I can't see what's wrong here; it's just wrong.

In the end, the correct piece of code was:
terah.children = {}

After sitting their for a good 5 minute in a loop of feeling like I need to cry - but not being able to,
Wanting to do anything else - but not being able to, as I also want to get this done,
Just sitting in my tiny space, feeling sad and stuck, I eventually gave in and tried the tools I knew how to use.
First,
Ai.

I feel rotten for using it as much as I did this week, But without it, I truly feel like I wouldn't have gotten anywhere and just made myself feel worse.

First I copied and pasted the question into gemini, asking it to please break down what it is the question is asking me to do, using other words.
When it spat out;
“Can you modify the terah information to include a new section called "children"? This new section should be empty for now.”

This immediately made more sense and between the two, I could understand mostly what it wanted me to do.

I was still stuck on what the heck “object literal notation” was, so I asked,
“For this problem, what would “object literal notation be?”

It said;
“Object literal notation: This is a fancy way of saying how we write the information for the “terah” box. It involves curly braces {} and colons :”

Great so this whole time I was duped by fancy words that mean the same thing as “key value pairs.”

It felt like my brain just returned static no matter what part I searched or read, For awhile, it all felt like French going in one ear and out the other. My brain still feels like a dumb, slow, sieve.

After trying a bunch of different orders of the different names and parts, one worked!.

I don't think I learned much about the code, as, thinking back, I can't remember ANY of it.
But I learned more about using Google and Gemini to make things make sense to me.
I learned that if I'm not careful, Gemini will try to just give me the piece of code.

That's not useful. I can't use that, so now I have to ask it to scrape that and ask it to help me understand it another way as we're not allowed to use it's code as per our policy.

I do wonder if this is an accurate representation of the industry or not,
I see that we all want to make the best use of our time so if we can use ai to make the base part of the smaller chunks of code, as long as we understand what it is and how it is doing what it does,
(which I don't yet, so I understand this is why we can't use it yet.)

If it saves us from typing for hours, would that not be a good tool to use? And what if it can teach us about better tools to use than the ones we knew about? That's really awesome.

Of the other problem solving methods, I have also tried using pseudocode.
It feels just like how I take my notes, I didn't notice any difference. It still works great for mapping out what I need to do.

I was “Trying something” all the time, like a loop, “try this, that part works, that part not yet, try that part again,” looping until it works.

I tried the rubber ducky method on the first problem and the second to last problem in our javascript kata modules, using my little tiger soft toy I have sitting near my desk.
For the first one, I caught my own syntax fix and boom, it worked!

For the second to last one, no matter how many times I read it out loud or compared it to the notes from one of our mentors, Gerard, I couldn't see any reason for it to be failing.

In the end for this one, I copied and pasted the code into our after hours discord chat channel and a guy called Pete found my missing quotation marks.

Reading error messages wasn't any help to me; it all sounded like French.
I tried the same thing that had saved me before asking Gemini to explain it in different, more basic words, but that made it more confusing.

It asked for more details, so I gave it the question and instead of continuing to break it down,
It immediately returned to the top of a text box, I recognized that box from earlier and quickly exited before I could spoil it for myself.

The question was hard enough. I didn't want to have to find an alternative, probably harder way to solve the question, just because I asked for help from AI incorrectly.

Console.logging, I do not understand this at all, Amiee and I are going to spend time going over this together tonight/monday.

Googling doesn't help as much as I thought it would, I have found google's AI way more useful when searching for “what is…….”

Asking my peers for help, Amiee and I work together and bounce ideas off each other.

Asking coaches for help is tricky; half the time I don't even know what is tripping me up, so by the time I've worked that out, I'm on a roll.

Improving your process with reflection, This is the one thing I didn't do enough of this week, and I reckon it's what mostly made my stress levels this week different from the rest.

I spent more time this week practicing meditation than journaling, and since I've been journaling for such a long time, maybe this was also a bit of a shock to my system to not work through the feelings in my head as much.

In summary,
I feel stupid.
I'll be okay.
I'm thankful for Gemini, sewing, and my family.
