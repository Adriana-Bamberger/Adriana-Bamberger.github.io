The Difference Between Padding, Border and Margin.
To help understand these three, I'll use a analogy of a painting on a gallery wall.
As the image below depicts. Our gallery wall is our webpage.
Our image is in a picture frame, A picture frame is made up of;

  • A matte or white piece of cardstock that spaces out the image from the usually pretty wooden picture frame. We call this "padding.”
  • The distance from the edges of the gallery wall (the red lines) to where the edges of the picture frame are is the "margin.”
  • he pretty picture frame is its "border.”
