BootCamp Learning Plan

Core - Sprint 2 - 31/3/2024 - Dev Academy Foundations

I have a short-term goal and a long-term idea.
In five years, I would love to be able to work at least part time remotely, learn new things, and be employed by a company that is positively impacting the world. In five years or more, anything could happen but I would like to spend some time in the Austrian alps, working in the mornings and skiing in the evenings.

I don't believe my strengths and limitations have changed that much.
I still have determination to prove myself.
I still love challenges.
My strong sense of justice is still in tact.
I'm definitely still extroverted in that I love meeting new people and making new friends and connections.
I'm decisive,
I still have and value direct communication.

My limitations are similar; I can still be blunt and/or stubborn.
I still lack strong sense of focus I can get distracted easily.
I still struggle to detect sarcasm.
And even though I love my strong sense of justice, this could still come back to bite me.

My biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be my tendency to get distracted at the smallest thing, If I'm not fully interested in what I've sat down to do, my brain searches for serotonin triggers, leading to wandering thoughts of costume designs, fabrics, business ideas, fun colors, patterns and textures in the environment around me, quite literary anything, even a blank wall.

In bootcamp, I'd love to work on pair coding, giving and receiving helpful kind feedback, and Improving my concentration.

My expectations from the team have not changed.
I expect support when I ask for it, a watchful eye to make sure I'm on track, and creative, constructive input always.
I also expect everyone to have different styles of humor. I'm looking forward to meeting everyone in person.


During bootcamp, I expect to do the following to help myself stay on track with the fast-paced work.
Pre-planning out my time in my google calendar as per our given schedule with time blocking,
During the day, using toggler to measure the actual time I spent of different things,
And the end of the day, I go back and adjust my calendar to what I actually did. (This helps me see my patterns in work flow, what I'm faster at vs. slower at and my progress.)

To keep my self from getting distracted when I think of something distracting I will pause my timer if I think it will take more than a a minute to type, I have a preset quick button on my stream deck to pull up my "to do later" list in notion, quickly type it out their and then close the window. I've noticed that this has been able to effectively solve the random ideas getting me distracted problem so far.

To keep up and seek help in a timely way I will be conscious to make sure I am staying on track with time allotted so that I don't work overtime, and reach out in the appropriate channels before it comes to that.

Most importantly, I will ask for help as soon as I have spent 10 minutes trying to work it out by myself or I realize google can not help me - whichever comes first.
In so many words, I can use "Hey [persons-or-groups-name], would you be able to help me to…."
By putting the allotted times into my Google Calendar, I can see them in Toggler next to the actual current tracking time. This way, I can see in the same screen how I'm progressing.
